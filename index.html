Why this exists

I built this guesthouse with my own hands in a village most Chinese people have never heard of.

It is, of course, also a business.
But more importantly, it gives people the chance to slowly discover a more real China through everyday life.

I've watched China get explained, flattened, and packaged for foreign audiences for years — the skylines, the tech, the approved narratives. What rarely makes it through is the other 90% of the country: the villages, the markets, the Tuesday mornings where nothing particular happens and everything is quietly real.

I want to change that. Not by telling you what to think. But by giving you a place to stay long enough to see for yourself.

This is not a hotel. It's not a tour.
It's an experiment — and you're part of it.

— Zayn, founder & builder

What a month here looks like

Four scenes from daily life

Garden

The vegetables on your plate came from twenty meters away.

You can harvest them yourself, or help plant the next batch. The seasons here are not a metaphor — they run the kitchen. Whatever is ready is what you eat. Nothing is shipped in. Everything has a name.

Market

The town market opens every day. Five kilometers away.

Vendors who've known each other for decades. Prices written on cardboard. No English anywhere. Bring a translation app, or just point and smile. The market doesn't perform for visitors — it just runs, the way it always has.

Neighbors

The people next door are not performers of rural life.

They farm, argue, cook, and watch TV — or try to, until the remote gets accidentally set to a menu they can't navigate back from. If you're good with your hands, you'll find yourself useful here. A satellite dish that lost its signal. A roof tile that shifted after rain. An elderly woman holding out her phone, asking you to help her order a washing machine online.

You won't be paid for any of this. What you'll get is a meal that took three hours to make, a thumbs-up delivered with complete sincerity, and the specific feeling of being genuinely needed somewhere you didn't expect.

Your space

Stable WiFi. Quiet mornings. Zero tourist infrastructure.

The kind of footage nobody else has — because nobody else stayed. The house has twelve rooms, a shared kitchen, a reading room with more books than you'll finish, and a view that makes it hard to sit down and work. You'll sit down anyway. The mountains will still be there.

The other side of this

This isn't only about solitude.

It's also about the people in the house with you. Each cohort brings together a small group — creators, filmmakers, writers, photographers — people who are curious about the world and practiced at expressing it. Think of it less like a guesthouse, more like the best hostel you've ever stayed in: the kind where you end up talking until 2am, collaborating on something you didn't plan, and leaving with three friendships you didn't expect. The village provides the material. The people in the house provide the energy.
